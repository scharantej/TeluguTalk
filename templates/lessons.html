Telugu Lessons

Welcome to the Telugu lessons. We'll guide you through the basics of the language, starting with common phrases and pronunciations.

Common Telugu Phrases

  • Hello: నమస్తే (namaste)
  • Thank you: ధన్యవాదములు (dhanyavadamulu)
  • Goodbye: వీడ్కోలు (veedukolu)
  • Yes: అవును (avunu)
  • No: కాదు (kadu)

Pronunciation

Telugu has a unique alphabet and pronunciation system. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • The letter "a" is pronounced like the "a" in "apple".
  • The letter "i" is pronounced like the "i" in "ink".
  • The letter "u" is pronounced like the "oo" in "boot".
  • The letter "e" is pronounced like the "e" in "egg".
  • The letter "o" is pronounced like the "o" in "old".

Basic Telugu Grammar

Telugu grammar is relatively straightforward. Here are some essential concepts to get you started:

  • Telugu is a subject-verb-object language.
  • Nouns have two genders: masculine and feminine.
  • Verbs have three tenses: present, past, and future.
  • Adjectives come after the nouns they modify.
